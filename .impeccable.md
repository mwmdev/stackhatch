## Design Context

### Users
StackHatch is for solo founders, small technical teams, and small agencies who need to turn repositories, product ideas, or requirements into clear architecture maps and handoff artifacts. They are evaluating technical direction quickly and need confidence that the tool understands real engineering tradeoffs.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel warm, technical, and credible. It should communicate practical engineering confidence without feeling cold, enterprise-heavy, or ornamental.

### Aesthetic Direction
Minimalistic but warm, with an earthy gruvbox-inspired palette: sandy neutrals, muted pastels, and grounded accent colors. The product should avoid typical AI-generated UI tropes such as generic blue/purple tech palettes, gradient text, glassmorphism, inflated hero metrics, repetitive icon-card grids, and decorative effects that do not support comprehension.

### Design Principles
- Lead with concrete product evidence: real architecture maps, real screencasts, and specific workflows should carry the page.
- Keep the surface quiet and useful: restrained layout, strong hierarchy, and copy that respects technical users' time.
- Use warmth through palette and texture, not decoration: sandy neutrals and muted accents should make the product feel approachable without weakening technical credibility.
- Avoid generic AI aesthetics: no gradient text, neon-on-dark, decorative glass panels, or templated feature-card rhythm.
- Prioritize confidence over hype: claims should be specific, verifiable, and tied to the job of explaining architecture decisions.
